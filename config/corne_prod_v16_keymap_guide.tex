\section{Overview}

This keymap is designed for a 42-key Corne split keyboard running ZMK
firmware on Nice!Nano controllers. It has four active layers and one
reserved template layer.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{cll}
\toprule
\textbf{Layer} & \textbf{Name}    & \textbf{How to activate} \\
\midrule
0              & Default          & Always active \\
1              & Lower            & Hold left thumb \texttt{BSPC} key; or tap rightmost thumb key to toggle \\
2              & Raise            & Hold right thumb \texttt{SPC} key \\
3              & Func             & Hold rightmost thumb key \\
4              & Template         & Unused / reserved \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent\textbf{Changes from v15:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Lower layer F key position now sends \texttt{Ctrl+B}.
  \item Rightmost thumb key: tap = toggle Lower layer, hold = momentary Func layer (was plain \texttt{Func} hold only).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Special behaviors}

\begin{tabular}{lp{9cm}}
\toprule
\textbf{Key} & \textbf{Behavior} \\
\midrule
\texttt{TAB / LCTRL}      & Tap = Tab; hold = Left Control (mod-tap) \\
\texttt{ESC / LSHFT}      & Tap = Escape; hold = Left Shift (mod-tap) \\
\texttt{BSPC / RALT}      & Tap = Backspace; hold = Right Alt (mod-tap) \\
\texttt{`}                & Grave / backtick (lower-left, always) \\
\texttt{BSPC / LWR}       & Tap = Backspace; hold = activate Lower layer \\
\texttt{SPC / RSE}        & Tap = Space; hold = activate Raise layer \\
\texttt{LWR + GUI}        & Hold = Lower layer \emph{with} Left GUI held simultaneously (single-hand \texttt{Cmd+number} shortcuts) \\
\texttt{TOG\_LWR / FUNC}  & Tap = toggle Lower layer on/off; hold = momentary Func layer \\
\texttt{;}                & 1 tap = \texttt{;}; 2 taps = one-shot Shift (next key is shifted) \\
\texttt{,}                & 1 tap = \texttt{,}; 2 taps = one-shot Ctrl (next key gets Ctrl) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\begin{tabular}{lp{9cm}}

\begin{tabular}{lp{9cm}}

\texttt{HUI}  & Increase hue (shift color toward red $\to$ yellow $\to$ green \ldots). \\

\section{One-Shot Keys (tap-dance)}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{clll}
\toprule
\textbf{Key} & \textbf{1 tap} & \textbf{2 taps} & \textbf{Tapping window} \\
\midrule
\texttt{;} & Semicolon & One-shot Shift & 200\,ms \\
\texttt{,} & Comma     & One-shot Ctrl  & 200\,ms \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

A \emph{one-shot} modifier applies to the very next key pressed, then
releases automatically. Double-tap within 200\,ms to activate.
